Common Medical Information

1. First Aid Basics
- For minor cuts and scrapes: Clean the wound with soap and water, apply antibiotic ointment, and cover with a sterile bandage.
- For burns: Cool the burn under running water for 10-15 minutes, do not pop any blisters, and cover with a sterile bandage.
- For nosebleeds: Sit upright, lean forward, and pinch the soft part of the nose for 10-15 minutes.

2. Common Symptoms and When to Seek Medical Attention
- Fever above 103°F (39.4°C) in adults
- Severe headache with stiff neck
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Chest pain or pressure
- Sudden dizziness or confusion
- Severe or persistent vomiting

3. Basic Health Guidelines
- Get 7-9 hours of sleep per night
- Drink 8 glasses of water daily
- Exercise for at least 30 minutes most days
- Eat a balanced diet with plenty of fruits and vegetables
- Maintain regular check-ups with your healthcare provider

4. Medication Safety
- Always read medication labels carefully
- Take medications as prescribed
- Store medications in a cool, dry place
- Keep medications out of reach of children
- Never share prescription medications

5. Emergency Preparedness
- Keep a first aid kit at home and in your car
- Know the location of the nearest emergency room
- Have emergency contact numbers readily available
- Keep a list of current medications and allergies
- Know basic CPR and first aid procedures

6. Mental Health Awareness
- Common signs of stress: irritability, sleep problems, changes in appetite
- Anxiety symptoms: rapid heartbeat, sweating, difficulty concentrating
- Depression symptoms: persistent sadness, loss of interest, changes in sleep patterns
- When to seek help: if symptoms persist for more than two weeks
- Available resources: hotlines, mental health professionals, support groups

7. Preventive Care
- Regular physical examinations
- Vaccination schedules
- Cancer screenings
- Dental check-ups
- Eye examinations
- Blood pressure monitoring
- Cholesterol checks

8. Healthy Lifestyle Tips
- Maintain a healthy weight
- Limit alcohol consumption
- Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke
- Practice stress management techniques
- Stay physically active
- Get regular health screenings
- Maintain good hygiene practices

9. Common Medical Terms
- Hypertension: high blood pressure
- Diabetes: condition affecting blood sugar levels
- Asthma: chronic respiratory condition
- Arthritis: joint inflammation
- Migraine: severe headache often with other symptoms
- Anemia: low red blood cell count
- Osteoporosis: weakened bones

10. When to Call Emergency Services
- Unconsciousness
- Severe bleeding
- Suspected heart attack
- Suspected stroke
- Severe burns
- Poisoning
- Severe allergic reactions
- Seizures
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe trauma
